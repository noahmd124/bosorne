About the book


Set against the backdrop of World War II, "A Flower in Winter" follows the journey of 13-year-old Samuel De Souza. As Germany invades Holland in May 1940, Samuel, a Jewish boy, is given a forged passport and smuggled out of the country under the care of two elderly strangers. Arriving in England, he finds solace and guidance from Jannie, one of his caretakers who becomes a mentor and father figure. Samuel's life takes a turn when he falls in love with Annie. However, driven by the atrocities happening in his homeland and the personal loss he experiences, he lies about his age to enlist in the Royal Navy, determined to stand against the oppressive regime threatening his people. The narrative delves into themes of love, honor, duty, and bravery, drawing inspiration from real events. The title is derived from a Portuguese proverb: "The love of an old man is like a flower in winter."
