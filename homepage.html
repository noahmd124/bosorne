About the book

In May 1940, as Germany invades Holland, 13-year-old Samuel De Souza is thrust into a perilous journey. With a forged passport and under the care of two elderly strangers, he is smuggled out of his homeland, narrowly escaping the Nazis. Arriving in England, Samuel finds refuge and a sense of belonging. Among newfound friends, he discovers the unwavering guidance of Jannie, one of the elderly men who becomes not only a mentor but a father figure. Life seems to offer him hope—especially when he meets and falls in love with Annie. But tragedy strikes. Enraged by the devastation in his homeland, the suffering of his people, and the death of Annie’s mother in a German air raid, Samuel takes a bold step: he lies about his age to join the Royal Navy, determined to fight against the regime threatening everything he holds dear. Through the trials of war, Samuel remains in contact with Jannie, realizing the profound power of love, courage, and human connection that transcends generations and cultures. From quiet moments of friendship to the storm of battle, he stands resolute against oppression, guided by honour and bravery. A story inspired by real events, A Flower in Winter explores love, duty, and the enduring strength of the human spirit. "The love of an old man is like a flower in winter." — Portuguese proverb
